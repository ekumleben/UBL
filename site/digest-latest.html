Subject: D2 resident input on FY 2026-27/27-28 budget priorities Dear Budget & Appropriations Committee Members, I'm writing as a District 2 resident to provide input on the two-year budget before your June 25 vote. I want to flag three contested line items where I'd appreciate seeing the committee's priorities: **Free City College cash grants (30% proposed cut):** These direct cash grants remove a major barrier to community college completion. If the goal is workforce development and economic mobility, cutting the grant program seems counterproductive—especially when the city is already covering tuition through the College Promise. **DPH community clinic closures (3 sites):** Closing neighborhood clinics pushes more patients into emergency rooms, which costs the city more in the long run and reduces preventive care access. I'd like to understand what the alternative access plan is for residents currently served by these sites. **Nonprofit contract reductions (pushing ~1,000 layoffs):** The city relies on nonprofit partners to deliver direct services. Large-scale layoffs will reduce service capacity just as we're seeing demand stay high. What's the plan for maintaining service levels with fewer staff? I understand the deficit is real, but these three cuts seem to create more expensive problems downstream. I'd urge the committee to restore these line items during the add-back process if revenue allows. Thank you for your work on this budget. [Your name] [Your address — include to confirm D2 residency]

Lurie/Mahmood foreclosure tax measure likely headed to November ballot—early window to shape it

📋 In Committee

Mayor Lurie and Supervisor Mahmood introduced a ballot measure to tax foreclosure sales (eliminating current exemptions), potentially raising $200M for the city's budget deficit. This will likely qualify for the November 2026 ballot. The measure is still being drafted—specifics on rate, exemptions, and use of funds are still malleable.

You're in Mahmood's neighboring district. This is the early stage where constituent input can still shape the details—tax rate, carveouts, how the money gets spent. Email your supervisor (stephen.sherrill@sfgov.org) and Mahmood (bilal.mahmood@sfgov.org) now with your position on the principle and specifics. Once it qualifies for the ballot (probably by late summer), the text is locked and you're down to yes/no. Early comment = higher leverage.
