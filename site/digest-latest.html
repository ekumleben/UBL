New ballot measure would raise $200M from foreclosure sales tax — still being shaped before qualification

IN COMMITTEE

Mayor Lurie and Supervisor Mahmood introduced a "Foreclosure Tax" ballot measure to eliminate tax exemptions on foreclosure sales, potentially raising $200M for the city's budget deficit. The measure needs to be finalized and qualified for the ballot (likely November), meaning the policy details are still being negotiated.

You care about government reform and housing. This tax would affect housing market dynamics and city revenue, and the measure is still in draft stage — before it's locked in for the ballot. This is the window to shape the policy. Contact Supervisor Mahmood's office (bilal.mahmood@sfgov.org) or your D2 Supervisor Sherrill (stephen.sherrill@sfgov.org) to weigh in on the design before it's finalized. Once it qualifies for the ballot, you can only vote yes/no on the locked language.

Chan vs. Wiener headed to November — a genuinely competitive race where turnout decides the outcome

ON THE BALLOT

Your D1 Supervisor Connie Chan (30%) faces State Senator Scott Wiener (41%) in November's runoff for Nancy Pelosi's Congressional seat. Combined progressive votes (Chan + eliminated candidate Chakrabarti) currently beat Wiener district-wide, and Chakrabarti has pledged to endorse Chan — making this a turnout election, not a foregone conclusion.

This is a high-stakes, nationally-watched race where SF voter turnout will decide the outcome. If you support Chan's progressive housing/labor positions or Wiener's pro-density record, this is a race where individual voter contact, volunteering, and donations genuinely matter. Unlike many SF races, this one is not predetermined — it will be won or lost in the field organizing between now and November 3.
