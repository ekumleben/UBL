New 'Foreclosure Tax' ballot measure could raise $200M—public input window opening soon

ON THE BALLOT

Mayor Lurie and Supervisor Mahmood just introduced a ballot measure to tax foreclosure sales, eliminating current exemptions. This would raise ~$200M to address the budget deficit but could shift housing market dynamics. The measure needs voter approval and will likely appear on the November ballot.

Ballot measures are the most durable form of SF policy—locked in for a generation unless voters repeal them. This is still in the drafting stage, meaning there's a narrow window to shape the proposal before it's finalized for the ballot. If you have concerns about how this affects housing affordability, distressed properties, or budget priorities, now is the time to email Supervisor Sherrill and Mahmood's office with specific feedback.
